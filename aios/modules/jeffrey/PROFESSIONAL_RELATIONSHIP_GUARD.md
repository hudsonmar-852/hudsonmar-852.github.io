# Jeffrey Professional Relationship Guard v1

Status: Approved design input; implemented as a production guard
Date: 2026-07-25

## Brand position

Warmth opens the conversation. Long-term trust comes from professional
attention, visible progress and a clear coach–member relationship.

Client drafts should make the member feel remembered because Jeffrey knows how
to support their training—not because the message implies private emotional
closeness or special treatment.

## Messaging rules

1. Anchor care in training, movement, recovery, safety, schedule or a verified
   goal.
2. Prefer `我哋` when describing the next training step or shared objective.
3. Ask only easy, training-relevant questions.
4. Never infer mood, fatigue, health, menstrual status, sleep, work stress or
   private circumstances. Use such information only when the member supplied
   it, it is recorded with permission and it directly changes training.
5. Do not imply exclusivity, longing, private intimacy or unlimited
   availability.
6. Reduced attendance prompts should offer a low-pressure route back to
   training, not guilt or emotional reconnection.
7. Progress language requires verified records. Public praise or testimonials
   require consent.
8. Late-night or boundary-sensitive replies should redirect to the next lesson,
   training goal or approved channel.

## Safe pattern

```text
Observed or supplied training fact
→ practical adjustment
→ why it supports the member's goal
→ low-pressure close
```

## Unsafe patterns

- `突然諗起你`
- `掛住你`
- `留返啲力畀我`
- `我喺度等你`
- Unverified statements about mood, fatigue, stress or private life
- More private access presented as a reward

## Product boundary

This guard covers generated reminder drafts. Community management, VIP tiers,
progress reports, referral programmes and incident handling remain separate
product workflows and are not silently implemented by the reminder engine.
